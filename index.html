About:

I am a multifaceted creative with a background in music, secondary through graduate level education, non profit management, and front end web development. Above all, I strive to always push myself by learning new skills and continually following my interests and passions.

I created my first website in 2006 while working as a freelance musician. Since then, I have encountered so many professional and personal projects requiring web development, content, and social media that I decided to take a course in Front End Web Development in 2014 to solidify my skill set. I found the course to be life changing, and I have been working full time in web development ever since.

HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, javascript, MEAN, Ruby, Ruby on Rails, Sinatra, PostgreSQL, Mongo, Photoshop, Illustrator, Git. In addition to these skills, I have almost two years of full time experience as a front end developer.

Prior to starting the full time Web Development Immersive at General Assembly, I worked as a Front End Web Developer for a fast paced tech startup in Atlanta. This position included javascript, jQuery, HTML, CSS, Photoshop, and T-SQL. In this

Front End Development hooked me because it gives me the same rush as a musical performance. One can spend the time to craft the code and end up with an application that is a creative output. The beauty lies in capturing a designer's vision from their mockup into a responsive, usable web application. I thrive under pressure, and I enjoy change.

I am a lifelong learner, and I enjoy the challenge of learning new skills. As I progressed as a front end developer, I kept running into the desire to make my own full stack applications. I have an internal drive to always excel and learn new skills.
